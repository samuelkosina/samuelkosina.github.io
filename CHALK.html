Urban Forensics
2025


This project investigates the traces we leave behind in the urban environment. I collected discarded objects from the street, cleaned them, and traced their outlines with chalk directly on the sidewalk where they were found. The method reminds of forensic practices — marking the presence of what is normally ignored — turning forgotten debris into visible evidence.


By highlighting these temporary traces, the project draws attention to the everyday material and leftover objects of the city. It transforms overlooked garbage into temporary markers of human activity, inviting passersby to notice the small, often invisible impacts of consumption and abandonment. The work operates in the gap between disappearance and presence: the objects are removed, but their outlines remain. It is a form of quiet reflection, making visible the silent narratives of urban life that typically go unnoticed.
